# Project Vision

## Game Identity

`strange-path` is a pixel-art soulslike where the player explores a humongous,
interconnected map, fights dangerous enemies and bosses, and gradually pieces
together the world's history without direct exposition.

The game should feel lonely, deliberate, hostile, and discoverable. The player is
not meant to be told exactly what to do. NPCs can steer the player in a useful
direction, but they should not explain the full plot, objective chain, or world
truth.

## Player Experience

The player should:

- Read danger from animation, enemy placement, arena shape, and sound.
- Discover routes through landmarks, locked paths, shortcuts, and odd details.
- Learn boss behavior through repeated attempts.
- Feel that death is part of understanding the world, not a random punishment.
- Build a mental map over time.
- Suspect that every place has a reason to exist.

## Design Pillars

- Precise combat matters more than system quantity.
- The world should guide through structure, not explicit instruction.
- Bosses should be readable, punishing, and fair.
- Small discoveries should make the map feel ancient and intentional.
- Mystery should come from omission and implication, not confusion.

## Avoid

- Tutorial-heavy onboarding.
- Quest markers that directly reveal the next objective.
- Long NPC exposition dumps.
- Random-feeling enemy placement.
- Adding RPG systems before the core combat loop feels good.
- Huge content production before the engine demo proves the feel.
